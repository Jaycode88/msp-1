Frequently Asked Questions

It is mostly important that you keep you hair healthy and clean. If you have any semi-permanent styling like braids or cornrolls etc, It will save time if you have already brushed this out and washed the hair. I strongly reccomend not using any conditiong products on your hair for at least a week before your dreading journey begins, As this leaves the hair silky and harder to lock.

To keep your dreadlocks clean and odour free, You can wash them between once a week to once a month.It is important not to use shampoos that will leave behind residues, As these can build up and cause mould inside your locks and other nasty problems. It is recommended not to wash your dreadlocks for the first 4-6 weeks from having them put it. It is very important to ensure you dry your dreadlocks thoroughly after washing. Towel rubbing is not the best method of drying as this can leave a fuzzy look, As it pulls all the shorter hairs at the root from the dreadlock.

The best way for you to avoid as many maintainance visits is to keep a good schedule of maintainance yourself. The easiest method is to daily seperate any locks that have hairs joining between them, This is done by pulling the dreads apart. Daily palm rolling will also help a lot, Once you have seperated your locks roll the seperatly between your palms. If you want to go further you can order a set of crochet hooks and learn how to pull the loose hairs into the dreadlock using a crochet hook.

Colouring can be done before or after having dreadlocks created, How ever it is much easier to do so before if you already know what colour you want and of course it can be changed again at a later stage after locking. Much caution is advised with bleaching hair before dreadlocks as if not done correctly it can make the hair very fine and weak which is no where near ideal for creating dreadlocks.

It is best to have hair atleast 6 inches in length, Any less than this and it is extremely difficult to get the hair to lock. If you have shorter hair but want longer dreadlocks, You have the option to get extensions. I strongly advise only using real natural hair for extensions and not synthetic.
